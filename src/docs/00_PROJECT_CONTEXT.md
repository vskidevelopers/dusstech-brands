# Dusstech Brands Platform

## Project Vision

We are building a modern digital platform for **Dusstech Brands Limited**, a branding, printing, and signage company based in Nairobi, Kenya.

This is **not** a simple business website.

It is a complete Branding Business Management Platform consisting of three connected experiences:

1. Admin Portal
2. Public Website
3. BrandFlow (Guided Customer Experience)

The platform should be scalable, production-ready, highly modular, and reusable for future branding businesses.

# Primary Business Goal

The purpose of the platform is to convert visitors into customers.

The platform should encourage users to:

- Browse services
- Browse products
- Request quotations
- Place orders
- Contact the business
- Start WhatsApp conversations

The website is a sales tool.

It is **not** an inventory management system.

# Core Business Models

Dusstech Brands operates two different business models.

## Services

Services are performed on customer-owned items.

Examples:

- Jersey Branding
- Mug Branding
- T-shirt Branding
- Embroidery
- Window Graphics
- 3D Signage
- Graphic Design

Customer already owns jerseys.

Dusstech brands them.

Price:

KES 400 per jersey.

# BrandFlow

BrandFlow is the signature experience of the platform.

Instead of immediately asking users whether they want Services or Shop, the homepage asks:

"What would you like to brand today?"

Users select categories such as:

- Clothing
- Drinkware
- Business
- Signage
- Printing
- Corporate Gifts

BrandFlow progressively guides users until the platform understands whether they need:

- Branding Services

or

- Ready-made Products

BrandFlow should feel like an onboarding experience rather than navigation.

# Development Philosophy

Build one feature at a time.

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

Keep components reusable.

Keep folders modular.

Prioritize maintainability.

Prefer composition over duplication.

Always follow modern Next.js best practices.

Assume this project may later become a reusable SaaS platform.
